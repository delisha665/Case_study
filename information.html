1. WHAT IS GOOGLE LENS?

    Google Lens is a visual search engine developed by Google that utilizes image recognition technology. It allows users to glean information from the physical world by analyzing images captured through their device's camera. With features such as object and text recognition, product search, language translation, and barcode scanning, Google Lens seamlessly integrates the digital and physical realms. Whether identifying landmarks, translating text in real-time, or providing details about products, Google Lens serves as a versatile tool, making information easily accessible through visual input.


  2. WHAT IS MOBILE NET?

    MobileNet is a convolutional neural network architecture designed specifically for efficient and lightweight performance on mobile and edge devices. Developed by Google, MobileNet aims to provide a balance between accuracy and computational efficiency, making it well-suited for applications with limited computational resources. This architecture is commonly used in tasks like image classification, object detection, and other computer vision applications on mobile devices, allowing for faster and more efficient processing of visual data without compromising accuracy.
